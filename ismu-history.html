ISMU-history

The Federal State Budgetary Educational Institution of Higher Education "Irkutsk State Medical University" of the Ministry of Health of the Russian Federation (hereinafter - IGMU) is the first-born of higher medical education in the East of Russia and one of the oldest higher educational institutions in Siberia. It was opened on August 26, 1919 in the form of a medical department at the Faculty of Physics and Mathematics of Irkutsk State University. On January 20, 1920, the medical department was separated into an independent educational and administrative unit - the Medical Faculty of Irkutsk State University.

The elite of the Kazan Medical School stood at the origins of IGMU: Professor N.D. Bushmakin – the largest anatomist and organizer, rector of the university; Professor N.T. Shevyakov - a world-renowned biologist; Professor N.T. Sinakevich - a first-hand surgeon; Professor V.A. Donskoy - the founder of the Museum of Pathology and others. In the first year of the faculty's opening, the departments of normal anatomy with a museum and histology with a laboratory, pathological anatomy, bacteriology, operative surgery and topographic anatomy with a museum, general pathology, medical diagnostics (propaedeutics of internal diseases) began to function, the organization of the department of hospital surgery with a clinic was initiated. In 1921, departments with clinics began to function: obstetric and gynecological, faculty therapeutic and surgical with an X-ray room, propaedeutic and surgical with a laboratory, skin and venereal, eye diseases, nervous and mental diseases with an X-ray room, laboratory and library, physiology.

In 1921, a scientific medical society was organized at the Faculty of Medicine, in which, in addition to faculty members, practical doctors of the city and region took part. In 1922, the I.I. Mechnikov Student Scientific Society was established under the leadership of Professors A.A. Melkikh, V.G. Shipachev and O.I. Bronstein. Since 1923, the Irkutsk Medical Journal has been published. In 1925, the postgraduate school was established. The first graduate students were S.N. Sinakevich, T.A. Larionova and E.L. Mikhelson. A major event in the life of the faculty was the organization and holding in Irkutsk of the first congress of doctors of Eastern Siberia (1924). The Medical Faculty of the university occupied a large share in terms of personnel potential, scientific products and training. In 1928, the faculty had 24 professors, 46 associate professors and assistants, 21 residents and 11 graduate students. Out of 163 scientific papers published by the university – 40 papers on medical topics.

In 1929, a radical restructuring of higher education began to take place in the country. Medical universities were transferred from the Narkompros system to branch People's Commissariats. By this time, the internal need for transformation into an independent unit had also matured at the medical faculty. For this reason, in the spring of 1930, the Medical Faculty of Irkutsk University was separated into an independent institute and transferred to the People's Commissariat of Health.

In 1930, the Medical Faculty of Irkutsk State University became an independent institute. After its formation, the institute began to expand, in different years the sanitary-hygienic (now medical-preventive) - 1930, pharmaceutical - 1941, dental - 1952, pediatric faculties - 1982 were opened.

In 1936, the Institute was granted the right to defend dissertations for the degree of Candidate of Medical and biological Sciences, which contributed to the accelerated training of certified personnel to ensure the educational process and the development of science.

The Great Patriotic War posed the most difficult tasks for the staff of the Institute. Scientists of the Institute already in the first months of the war did a lot of work on the organization and methodological management of evacuation hospitals. The chief physician of faculty clinics A.A. Popov headed the department of evacuation hospitals of the regional health department. The main specialists of this department were scientists of the Institute: Professor A.I. Sorkina (chief surgeon), Professor N.Z. Mochalin (chief therapist), H.G. Khodos headed the neuropsychiatric center, Professor I.M. Krukover – otorhinolaryngological, Professor Z.G. Frank-Kamenetsky – ophthalmological. Professors V.G. Shipachev, I.A. Sinakevich, associate Professor E.I. Melnikova worked in the department of evacuation hospitals. Methodical management of the activities of hospital pathologists was carried out by Professor V.A. Donskov, dentists - associate professor S.N. Levenson, radiologists - associate professor V.H. Kogan. The evacuation hospitals were headed by the teachers of the Institute, associate professors N.V. In 1992, Irkutsk State Medical Institute passed the state certification for the assessment of the quality of training of specialists, conducted by the State Inspectorate for the certification of educational institutions of the State Committee of the Russian Federation for Higher Education. According to the conclusion of the educational and methodological association for medical education, the university principle of education is implemented at the institute. Thus, the whole complex of qualities and characteristics made possible the transition of the medical institute (in May 1995) to a new status – "university". In 1997, 2002, 2007, 2012 and 2017, IGMU underwent regular comprehensive performance evaluations and certification. As a result of the state accreditation of IGMU (2012), the status of the university was confirmed, the certificate of state accreditation was issued by the Federal Service for Supervision of Education and Science. The Federal Service for Supervision in the Field of Education and Science has issued a license to carry out educational activities for the right to provide educational services.

According to the decree of the Government of the Russian Federation dated 23.03.2010 No. 395-r, the university was reorganized by joining the federal state institution of secondary vocational education "Irkutsk Medical School (Technical School)", with subsequent education on the basis of the attached separate structural subdivision of the Institute of Nursing Education. The IGMU has implemented and effectively operates a quality management system. In November 2011, IGMU successfully passed the certification check of the quality management system for compliance with the international quality standard ISO 9001:2008 and the national standard GOST ISO 9001-2011. According to the results of this inspection of IGMU, quality certificates were obtained by the Certification Association "Russian Register".

Structural support and quality management control is carried out through:

  • - collegial advisory body Quality Council (Chairman - Rector of IGMU);
  • - annual educational and methodological conference "Quality management system: experience and prospects";
  • - training seminars of IGMU employees;
  • - conducting internal audits;
  • - rating assessment of the departments' activities;
  • - the service of quality commissioners in each structural subdivision of IGMU.
